Why I chose these movies?

These are movies that I enjoyed as I got older. I was never much of a movie person growing up. I always preferred TV shows or books. But recently, I have become quite the cinephile and I've been fascinated by scripts with excellent writing or twists. I have discovered I have a particular fondness for sleuthing, which I uncovered as I developed my tastes in films.

Annihilation

A woman goes into the mysterious, reality-altering "Area X" to investigate what happened to her late husband. He had gone on an expedition there himself, and hadn't came out quite right. Almost like he wasn't himself anymore. Will her background in science allow her to solve the issue at hand or will it only exacerbate where others before her have failed?


Nine Days

Nine days to figure out who is fit to live and who will fade into nothingness. Our protagonist, Will, spends those days interviewing each soul to see who is fit to deserve a life. Each soul he spends time with leaves him haunted as the sands of time flow forward to the harrowing decision he must make. Will he make the right choice?


Hereditary

A family is haunted by a deep-seated curse in this chilling horror film. Mysteries come to life from generations before as they unravel what is tearing their family apart. These secrets begin to take shape as the mother plays sleuth and the son plays the fool. Find out if this family triumphs through madness.


Midsommar

A struggling young couple visits a small rural town in Sweden. They are having a mid-summer festival and have been invited to participate in the merriment. Unfortunately, they find themselves trapped in a pagan cult's rituals and schemes. Will they be turned against each other or fight against their fates?


Dune(2021)

This film is the science fiction novel by Frank Herbert brought to life for a second time. The movie follows a young baron as he struggles between fulfilling the religious prophecy set forth by the Bene Gesserit and his own destiny. Meanwhile he is stranded on Arrakis trying to conquer it to maintain spice control and House Atreides' status alongside it.


Scream(1996)

A classic slasher film from the 90s that borders more on dark comedy than horror. The story is about Sidney, whose mother died under brutal circumstances, is now reliving the experience as a killer is hunting her. The movie is packed with meta-humor about horror movies and their tropes, but more importantly, I've never seen a killer get beat up so many times.


Alien: Romulus

A recent remake of the Alien series from the 1980s. A rebellious group of teens go into space hoping to get rich. What they find is the original spaceship from the original movie, still infested with aliens that devour their host and burst out of their chest. As well as the fully-grown aliens with acid blood and near invincibility, seeking only nourishment in the form of flesh.


The Batman

Robert Pattison plays the role of the dark knight in this movie and does a stellar portrayal of earlier versions of Batman. He stalks his prey, The Riddler, throughout this film as he attempts to prevent him from continuing his murdering spree. Meanwhile, he must quell Catwoman's lust for revenge. But how can he? Especially since he hasn't come to terms with his own desire for vengeance.


Knives Out

Wealthy entrepreneur, Harlan Thrombey, has died under mysterious circumstances. It is up to the winsome Cajun gentleman, Benoit Blanc, to solve it. He will discover the culprit by untangling the story Harlan's greedy and conniving family have cooked up for him. But it's never the person you expect with this screenwriter. It inspires laughter in an awkward sort of fashion, but it has its own unique charm.


The Rocky Horror Picture Show

A freshly-engaged young couple get stranded in a rainstorm at an eccentric person's house. They'd like to leave, but they're forced to watch Dr. Frank create the most perfect man against their will. It's a musical with horror themes, but isn't quite horror per se. Just very inappropriate in general, but it's a good watch.


Coraline

A young girl has her entire life uplifted and moved to an old Victorian home with a small door that connects to another parallel world. A world where her parents are the perfect, doting caregivers who give her all the sweets she could want. Where her new neighbor has his mouth sewn shut. But this new realm has a dark side that's very interested in her.


Donnie Darko

Frank, your average gloomy teenager, is experiencing hallucinations that are making him act erratic and unstable. It whispers to him the secrets of time travel and murder. But more importantly, it warns him of the upcoming apocalypse. Will he be able to change fate or his psychological hallucinations?
